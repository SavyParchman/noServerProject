The International Space Station in real time
  • The ISS is the single most expensive object ever built. The cost of it has been estimated at over $120 billion.
  • Flight software exceeds 1.8 million lines of source code supported on the ground by a test environment comprised of another 3.3 million source lines of simulation software, and 11 million source lines of test scripts.
  • Just because you’re in space doesn’t mean you can’t get a computer virus. The 52 computers onboard have been infected more than once.
  • The first virus was a worm known as the W32.Gammima.AG, which started spreading by stealing passwords to online video games on Earth.
  • Most importantly, the ISS is also the symbol of the end of Cold War and international understanding. 68 countries have been involved on the research onboard the station.
